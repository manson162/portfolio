OneTime

The idea for OneTime came from the realization that most electrical manufacturers were not able to provide our parent company Electrika with high-quality product data that was needed for our estimating software.

It was clear that companies needed a better PIM and I was tasked to design OneTime.

I was appointed as the lead UX/UI designer and product manager for this challenging project, overseeing a team of five developers, and two people from the data team to assist with user research.

Project goal

The goal of this project was to radically rethink how manufacturers and retailers approached product information management. PIM software is not new, but most products in the market fall short with their user experience. With complex user journeys and the inability to allow users to make large changes, it was clear the market was ready for something new.

OneTime is designed to disrupt the competition!

Research methods

I started with a large amount of competitor research on the leading brands within the PIM space, including in-depth user tests within my team and external customers.

I also used the following research methods:

  • Detailed interviews
  • User testing
  • Personas
  • Customer journey mapping
  • Information architecture
  • Market research analysis

Core customer needs

After extensive research, I was able to identify three key areas of frustration with the existing apps within the market. Difficult setup, not being able to make changes fast enough (e,g to many steps) and not being able to export the data in a way that their customer needs.

I also identified that users always seemed to fallback to spreadsheets to answer all of their data problems, so it was clear I had to include a spreadsheet type interface that would allow users to move seamlessly between Excel and OneTime.

Product data created by the marketing team would need to be very different from the data provided by the project management team. The data also needed to be displayed differently depending on the sales channel so an easy way to manage this was essential.

Sketch My Ideas

After the first set of research is complete, I start by printing some blank device page templates to be completed first on my own and then in collaboration with my team.

We watch users tests, look at research data together and use the following techniques to better define the problem.

  • Affinity diagrams
  • Card sorts
  • Personas


We also use the Persuasive, Validation and UI Patterns card decks from UI Shop for inspiration.

Create & Test Prototypes

After the first stage of sketches was done, I jumped into building a medium-fidelity prototype in Adobe XD.

I preformed user tests on my team at first before speaking with the development team about the technical challenges that we might face.

I then did user tests with some of our existing customers and potential customers. Some of them loved it so much they purchased the software based on the high-fidelity prototype.

The feedback we gather from the user tests of the prototypes was invaluable in building a successful product.

Learnings

Though my research I was able to empathize with the people that managed product data. I could feel the challenges they faced every day and I honestly wanted to improve their work life, through the design of a easy to use PIM system.
