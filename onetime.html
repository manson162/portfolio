OneTime

OneTime is a product management system designed to give manufacturers and retailers better control of detailed product information, making it easy to upload, manage, and then share the right data with their customers.

My team and I were tasked with redesigning an ageing system with a new one-page Angular 7 .net core 2 web app with a focus on simplicity, speed, and mobile responsiveness.

  • My Role

    • User research, prototyping, UX/UI design, and creation and maintenance of a design system. In addition, I am currently the product manager of OneTime.

  • Step 1 - Understand

    • I first met with clients and preformed detailed interviews regarding the existing tool and processes.

    Some of the responses were very concerning; "I hate it when..." and "it's really annoying whenâ€¦". These strong feelings were shared by most users.

    These insights helped me develop my participant screener and perform extensive usability testing on the existing system.

  • Step 2 - Define

    • After conducting detailed research of the existing system and the competitors, my team and I reviewed all of the research and ran an affinity diagram session to see if we could define the problem.

    It was clear the overall information architecture needed wholesale improvements as users would often get lost in the menus.

    We noticed a large difference in understanding of product management processes, so we made sure to account for this in our personas.

  • Step 3 - Ideate

    • I then sketched out some ideas on how I might solve some of the outlined problems. One of the first improvements I made was to the complex top-level navigation, reducing the ten options down to three: "Import, Manage, and Share".

    Also, I was able to define some core concepts such as the data explorer and the product inspector.

  • Step 4 - Prototype

    • I then continued sketching to define major user flows, screen states, and key interactions. I then transferred these sketches to a low-fidelity digital prototype in Adobe XD to preform future user testing.

    Both myself and my team created some technical prototypes to test the feasibility and usability of ideas.

  • Step 5 - Validate

    • I identified that users found channels complex and needed further improvement, so I made changes to address this and re-tested.

    However, the overall feedback of "This is so much better" and "I feel more in control" is a great start.

    This project is due to complete in January 2020.
