Schneider Electric

Schneider Electric, one of the worldâ€™s biggest electrical manufacturers, needed to replace their outdated Lotus Notes estimating software.

The system needed to be created as both a native iPad app and a web app so engineers could work seamlessly between devices.

  • My Role

    • User research, prototyping, UX/UI design, and creation and maintenance of a design system. In addition, I worked as the product manager.

  • Step 1 - Understand

    • I first started by interviewing around 30 Schneider Electric field engineers and support staff.

    They told me how they "hated" using the lotus system as it required them to carry their slow, heavy laptops onsite; often they would have to "hack the system" to get it to work the way they wanted.

  • Step 2 - Define

    • After performing detailed research of the existing system, my team and I reviewed all of the research and ran an affinity diagram session of our own to see if we could define the problems.

    It was clear that some of the steps in the process needed to be removed to make the quote process as efficient as possible.

    Also, automation and clever defaults were essential, as users would often need to input data multiple times.

    We then defined personas for each user type, such as field engineers, back-office admins, regional and national managers, and directors.

  • Step 3 - Ideate

    • I then sketched out some ideas on how I might solve some of the outlined problems, but I also needed to consider how components would work on both mobile and desktop.

    One of the first improvements identified was offline catching of quotes and product data to allow engineers to go "Offline", which was essential on secure site visits.

  • Step 4 - Prototype

    • I then continued sketching to define major user flows, screen states, and key interactions. I then transferred these sketches to a low-fidelity digital prototype in Adobe XD to preform future user testing.

    Both myself and my team created some technical prototypes to test the feasibility and usability of ideas.

  • Step 5 - Validate

    • I identified that many of the processes completed by the sales engineers could be automated, allowing them to quickly create more quotes on-site than they ever had before. Also, providing contextual help text was welcomed by the user.
